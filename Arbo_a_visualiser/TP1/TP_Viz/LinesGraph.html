Details

The goal was to study the evolution over the years, from 2015 to 2021, of the happiness score of each country.
We chose a dynamic analysis that enables a better representation of this evolution. Colors indicate the region each country belongs to.

We can see that overall, scores maintain themselves around the same value for each country. But if we look closer we can make the following observations:

With no surprise, countries with the highest happiness score are those part of Western Europe and North America.

Countries within the Latin America and Caribbean region show a disparity of scores.

Afghanistan's score drops after 2016 and constitutes the lowest score among all countries.

Finland appears to be the country with the happiest population, year after year.

There are a few countries for wich scores aren't available (Qatar, Bhutan, Somalia or Syria).
