This work is based on the world happiness score, a data set available on the kaggle website.

It is a publication by the united nation sutsainable development solutions network and uses data from the Gallup World Poll.

Each year, 1000 representative people over 15 years old are polled in each country: people are asked to rank their live on a ladder going from 0 (worst) to 10 (best).This gives the happiness score.

Other factors are present in the dataset, such as health, economy, family support, freedom. They are not present in the initial survey but those factors are found to be correlated with the happiness score.
Our dataset contains data from year 2015 to 2020.

This work was done by Elisabeth Bourgeois, Amina Youssef and Audrey Vidal from Specialized Master "Big Data" years 2021-2022.
